# Debate over the Russian oil waiver

The temporary US waiver on Russian oil is presented as a case where sanctions policy becomes a tool of market management as well as coercion. The argument for the waiver focused on stabilizing energy markets during a Middle East shock, while critics stressed the risk of financing Moscow and weakening leverage.

## Key points

- The waiver was temporary and tied to wider energy turbulence.
- Supporters emphasized price stability and market functioning.
- Critics emphasized lost leverage and renewed revenue for Russia.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

The note is useful for questions about how coercive tools can be softened or reconfigured when market stability becomes politically urgent.
