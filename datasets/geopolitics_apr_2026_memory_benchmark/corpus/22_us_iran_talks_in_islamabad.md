# US-Iran talks in Islamabad

The Islamabad talks are framed as unusually important because they represented the highest-level direct contact in many years. Their failure therefore carries weight: the talks showed diplomatic movement, but not enough convergence to produce a deal.

## Key points

- The talks addressed ceasefire arrangements, nuclear questions, and economic issues.
- They were politically significant because they were unusually high level.
- They ended without agreement, underscoring persistent mistrust.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note supports benchmark questions about why diplomatic significance and diplomatic success are different things.
