# Manila's search for sanctions waivers

Manila's interest in sanctions waivers shows how coercive regimes can create pressure on third countries when supply shocks hit. In the corpus, sanctions policy is never just a bilateral tool; it also shapes the choices of vulnerable importers.

## Key points

- The Philippines explored whether it could secure permission to buy from sanctioned producers.
- Iran and Venezuela were the main examples mentioned.
- This behavior was driven by energy necessity rather than ideological alignment.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This file helps benchmark whether the system can connect sanctions policy to third-country adaptation.
