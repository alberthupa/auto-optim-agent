# How Middle East instability spills into Asia

The Asian spillover story in the corpus is anchored by import vulnerability and policy adaptation. The Philippines is the clearest case, but the broader point is that fuel insecurity can shape strategic choices across Asia even when the crisis begins elsewhere.

## Key points

- Import-dependent states face immediate exposure to Gulf disruptions.
- Energy vulnerability encourages waiver requests and tactical accommodation.
- The effect is geopolitical, not merely economic.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note should link strongly to the Philippines, Hormuz, and sanctions-waiver materials.
