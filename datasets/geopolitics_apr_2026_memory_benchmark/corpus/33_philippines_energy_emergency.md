# The Philippines energy emergency

The Philippines declared an energy emergency because it was acutely exposed to oil disruption coming from the wider Middle East crisis. This is one of the benchmark's clearest examples of distant conflict producing immediate domestic-policy pressure in another region.

## Key points

- The country relies heavily on oil imports from the Persian Gulf.
- Supply disruption created transport and economic risks.
- The resulting pressure pushed Manila toward unconventional policy options.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note should be strongly linked to Hormuz, energy security, and sanctions-waiver questions.
