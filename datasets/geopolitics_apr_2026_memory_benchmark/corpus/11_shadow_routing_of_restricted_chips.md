# Shadow routing of restricted chips

The corpus treats chip smuggling and shadow routing as a predictable response to unmet strategic demand. When export controls restrict formal access but buyers still view advanced compute as essential, commercial pathways tend to become more opaque rather than disappearing.

## Key points

- High-end chips are tied to AI capacity and advanced computing, which keeps demand politically important.
- Opaque intermediaries and rerouted hardware flows become more likely when legal channels close.
- This dynamic shows the limits of control regimes that do not also change the underlying incentive structure.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

Good ingest should link this note to export controls, AI compute competition, and gray-market behavior.
