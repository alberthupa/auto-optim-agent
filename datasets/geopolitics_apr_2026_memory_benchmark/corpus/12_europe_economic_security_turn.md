# Europe's economic-security turn

Europe in this benchmark is not portrayed as a passive bystander between Washington and Beijing. It is moving toward an economic-security framing in which industrial policy, procurement rules, and exposure to Chinese overcapacity are treated as strategic concerns.

## Key points

- Chinese overcapacity in vehicles, solar, wind, and semiconductors sharpens European defensive policy debates.
- Economic-security language reframes trade friction as a resilience problem rather than a narrow price problem.
- The shift aligns Europe more closely with some US concerns even when Brussels and Washington do not take identical measures.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note helps evaluate whether the memory system can distinguish classic trade policy from economic-security strategy.
