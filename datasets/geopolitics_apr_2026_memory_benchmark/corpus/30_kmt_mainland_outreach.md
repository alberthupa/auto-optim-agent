# KMT outreach to the mainland

The KMT visit to mainland China matters because the benchmark treats Taiwan not as a purely military question but as a domestic-political and signaling environment. Political outreach can influence perceptions of risk and future electoral positioning.

## Key points

- Cheng Li-wun's visit was notable because it was the first by a KMT chair in many years.
- The visit intersected with domestic political strategy ahead of future elections.
- Cross-strait engagement can carry symbolic meaning even without immediate policy change.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note checks whether the memory system can hold political and military dimensions together.
