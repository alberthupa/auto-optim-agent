# Energy security as a driver of foreign policy

The corpus repeatedly shows energy security acting as a foreign-policy driver rather than a background economic variable. States revise diplomatic options, sanctions preferences, and partnership choices when fuel supply becomes insecure.

## Key points

- Energy insecurity shapes alliance priorities and sanctions debates.
- Import-dependent states may seek waivers or accommodation to maintain supply.
- Regional conflict can therefore restructure foreign policy at a distance.
