# The Strait of Hormuz as a global energy chokepoint

Hormuz functions in this benchmark as one of the clearest examples of a strategic chokepoint. Disruption there affects shipping, fuel prices, alliance planning, and the policy choices of energy-importing states far beyond the Gulf.

## Key points

- The strait is central to global oil flows.
- Insecurity there drives up energy costs and changes political calculations elsewhere.
- The issue links regional conflict to global economic and alliance consequences.
