# Sanctions as coercion and market management

In this benchmark, sanctions do not operate as a simple on-off punishment tool. They are adjusted, waived, or tightened in response to wider market effects, which makes them simultaneously coercive instruments and market-management tools.

## Key points

- Waivers can be used to stabilize prices or supply under emergency conditions.
- The same flexibility can also weaken pressure on the target state.
- The tension between leverage and stability is a recurring pattern in the corpus.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This file helps benchmark whether the vault preserves policy tradeoffs instead of one-dimensional labels.
