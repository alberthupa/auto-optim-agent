# Gray-zone pressure on Taiwan's infrastructure

The corpus highlights suspected interference with critical infrastructure as a gray-zone tactic against Taiwan. The point is that pressure can target communications and social resilience, not only visible military assets.

## Key points

- Suspected sabotage or interference affected critical links such as undersea communications infrastructure.
- Gray-zone tactics blur the line between peace and open conflict.
- These actions create strategic pressure without requiring a full invasion.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

A strong ingest pass should preserve both the infrastructure angle and the doctrinal label of gray-zone coercion.
