# Why traceable supply chains matter

Traceability appears in the corpus as a practical response to strategic uncertainty. If governments and firms cannot map where inputs come from or where bottlenecks lie, they cannot manage sanctions risk, export controls, or material scarcity effectively.

## Key points

- Traceability helps reveal hidden dependencies and vulnerabilities.
- It supports both resilience planning and compliance with strategic controls.
- The concept matters in minerals, chips, and defense-related production.
