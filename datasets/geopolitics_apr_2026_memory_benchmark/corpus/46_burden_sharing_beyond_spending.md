# Burden sharing beyond spending targets

The corpus argues that burden sharing cannot be reduced to whether allies hit a spending threshold. The harder question is who provides what capabilities, in which theater, and under what political constraints when crises overlap.

## Key points

- Spending targets matter but do not settle strategic allocation questions.
- Maritime security, Ukraine support, and technological adaptation all compete for attention.
- Alliance politics therefore involves deployment choices as much as budget choices.

This note is useful for harder benchmark questions that test abstraction rather than quote retrieval.
