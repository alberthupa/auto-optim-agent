# Chinese vessel surge in the East and South China Seas

Large-scale vessel deployments are used in this corpus as an example of coercive presence short of outright war. Numbers matter because they make the pressure visible and hard to dismiss as routine background activity.

## Key points

- Roughly one hundred naval and coast guard vessels were reported in and around the East and South China Seas.
- The deployment was described as unusually large for the period.
- Such presence operations reinforce coercion without necessarily crossing into declared war.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

This note supports benchmark questions about regional maritime pressure and gray-zone behavior.
