# Rare-earth leverage in 2026

China's position in rare-earth processing gives it leverage that extends far beyond commodity pricing. In this corpus, rare earths are treated as a strategic pressure point because they feed defense manufacturing, semiconductors, electric vehicles, and other advanced industries.

## Key points

- Dependence on Chinese-controlled processing creates exposure even when mining is geographically diversified.
- Restrictions on rare earths can disrupt downstream sectors quickly because processing capacity is hard to replace.
- Western coordination on minerals is partly an attempt to reduce this single-point strategic vulnerability.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

A memory system should connect rare-earth leverage to semiconductors, clean-tech supply chains, and defense readiness rather than storing it as an isolated commodity note.
