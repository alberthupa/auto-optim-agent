# Paris talks and summit preparation

Talks in Paris are framed as tactical diplomacy designed to stabilize a structurally adversarial relationship. The benchmark uses them to show that dialogue can coexist with deep industrial, security, and technology conflict.

## Key points

- Officials sought to prepare the ground for a future Trump-Xi summit.
- The talks suggested both sides wanted some managed stability.
- They did not remove underlying frictions over chips, trade, and strategic competition.

## Why this matters for the benchmark

A good vault should connect summit preparation with the recurring pattern of tactical dialogue amid structural rivalry.
